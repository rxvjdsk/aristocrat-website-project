About The Aristocrat

Before it was a national landmark, The Aristocrat was just a bold dream rolling through Manila’s Luneta Park in a 1936 Studebaker van. Founded by Engracia "Aling Asiang" Cruz-Reyes—the ultimate matriarch and legendary "Mother of Filipino Cooking"—and her husband, Justice Alex A. Reyes, this culinary powerhouse was named with a touch of irony to prove a point: that soul-satisfying local comfort food absolutely deserved a spot at the grandest tables. Aling Asiang revolutionized the dining scene with three simple laws—mabilis, mura, and malinis (fast, affordable, and clean)—elevating native flavors into something fit for royalty. Today, third- and fourth-generation descendants of the Reyes clan still passionately protect this legacy, personally taste-testing kitchens daily so that our iconic, smoky Chicken Barbecue with Java Rice, rich Kare-Kare, and legendary adobo flying saucers taste exactly like childhood. Proudly wearing an official historical marker from the National Historical Commission of the Philippines at our flagship Roxas Boulevard branch in Malate, we don't just serve meals; we welcome you to a multi-generational table where decades of family milestones, midnight cravings, and shared Philippine history come alive.
